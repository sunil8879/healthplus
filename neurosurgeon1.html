Choosing a Neurosurgeon:

  • Sub-specialty is Crucial: Ensure surgeon has significant experience in the specific type of surgery needed (e.g., complex spine, pituitary tumor, aneurysm, DBS).
  • Diagnosis: Obtain a clear diagnosis confirmed by imaging (MRI/CT) and neurological assessment first.
  • Team Approach: Look for hospitals with strong neurosciences departments including neuro-anesthesia, neurology, critical care, radiology.
  • Technology: Availability of specific technology (robotics, navigation, Gamma Knife/CyberKnife) might be important for certain procedures.
  • Experience & Volume: Inquire about the surgeon's and hospital's volume for your specific procedure.
  • Second Opinion: Essential for major brain or spine surgery. Seek opinions from specialists in your condition.
