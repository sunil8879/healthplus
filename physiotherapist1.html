Choosing a Physiotherapist:

  • Your Condition: Match the therapist's specialty (Ortho, Neuro, Sports, Cardio-Pulmonary, Peds, Women's Health) to your needs.
  • Diagnosis: Physiotherapy is often most effective after a medical diagnosis from a relevant doctor (Orthopedist, Neurologist, etc.).
  • Techniques: Discuss their approach (Manual Therapy, Exercise Prescription, Electrotherapy, Taping).
  • Location & Sessions: Therapy usually requires multiple sessions, so convenience matters.
  • Credentials: Look for BPT (Bachelor's) and preferably MPT (Master's) in a relevant specialty.

Choosing a Physiotherapist:

  • Your Condition: Match the therapist's specialty (Ortho, Neuro, Sports, Cardio-Pulmonary, Peds, Women's Health) to your specific needs.
  • Diagnosis: Often, you'll be referred by a doctor (Orthopedist, Neurologist, etc.) who has diagnosed the underlying issue.
  • Techniques: Some therapists specialize in manual therapy, others in exercise prescription, electrotherapy, taping, etc. Discuss their approach.
  • Location & Sessions: Physiotherapy often requires multiple sessions, so convenient location is important.
  • Credentials: Look for BPT (Bachelor's) and preferably MPT (Master's) in a relevant specialty.
