Choosing a Pulmonologist:

  • Your Condition: Asthma, COPD, cough, breathing difficulty, sleep apnea suspicion, lung infection (like TB or pneumonia), ILD, or need for procedures like bronchoscopy.
  • Sub-specialty Need: For complex issues like ILD, pulmonary hypertension, severe asthma, complex sleep disorders, or interventional procedures, look for a specialist with focused expertise.
  • Diagnostic Tools: Check if the hospital/clinic has necessary facilities like PFT (Pulmonary Function Test), Bronchoscopy suite, Sleep Lab, CT Scan.
  • Inpatient Care: If your condition might require hospitalization or ICU care, consider the pulmonologist's hospital affiliation and critical care capabilities.
  • Referral: Your GP can often provide a referral to a suitable chest specialist.
