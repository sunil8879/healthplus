Choosing an Infertility Specialist:

  • Diagnosis: Understand the cause(s) of infertility (male/female factor, unexplained).
  • Sub-specialty Match: Ensure expertise relevant to your situation (PCOS, endometriosis, low sperm count, recurrent miscarriage).
  • Clinic Success Rates: Inquire about IVF/ICSI success rates (live birth rates per transfer), interpreting cautiously based on patient demographics.
  • Technology & Lab: The quality of the IVF lab and experience of embryologists are crucial.
  • Communication & Approach: Choose a team you are comfortable with, who explain procedures clearly.
  • Cost & Transparency: Understand the costs involved for consultations, tests, and procedures.

Choosing an Infertility Specialist:

  • Diagnosis: Understand the potential cause(s) of infertility (male factor, female factor, unexplained).
  • Sub-specialty Match: Does the specialist have expertise relevant to your specific situation (e.g., PCOS, endometriosis, low sperm count, recurrent miscarriage)?
  • Clinic Success Rates: Inquire about the clinic's success rates for procedures like IVF/ICSI, but interpret these cautiously as they depend heavily on patient demographics. Look for live birth rates per embryo transfer.
  • Technology & Lab: The quality of the IVF lab is crucial. Look for established centers with experienced embryologists.
  • Communication & Approach: Choose a doctor and clinic team you feel comfortable with, who explain procedures clearly and address your concerns.
  • Cost & Transparency: Understand the costs involved for consultations, tests, and procedures.
