A representative list of recognized specialists. Not an objective ranking. Verify MS/DNB/MCh qualifications & sub-specialty expertise independently.

Choosing an Orthopedic Surgeon:

  • Sub-specialty is Crucial: Ensure expertise in Joint Replacement (Knee/Hip), Spine Surgery, Sports Medicine (Arthroscopy), Trauma, Pediatric Ortho, Hand Surgery, Ortho Oncology, etc.
  • Diagnosis: Get a clear diagnosis first, possibly from a general orthopedist or physician.
  • Experience & Volume: Higher volume often correlates with better outcomes for complex procedures like joint replacement or spine surgery.
  • Hospital Facilities: Ensure the hospital has necessary infrastructure (ICU, rehab, OT setup).
  • Second Opinion: Essential for major surgeries like joint replacement or complex spine procedures.
