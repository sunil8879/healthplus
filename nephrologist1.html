Choosing a Nephrologist:

  • Identify Your Condition: CKD, AKI, hypertension-related kidney issues, diabetic nephropathy, kidney stones (often co-managed with urologists), protein/blood in urine, electrolyte imbalance, need for dialysis, or kidney transplant evaluation?
  • Sub-specialty: For transplant, complex glomerular diseases, pediatric kidney issues, or specific procedures, seek a nephrologist with that focus.
  • Dialysis Needs: If requiring dialysis, consider the proximity and reputation of the dialysis center associated with the nephrologist.
  • Transplant Program: If considering transplant, evaluate the experience and outcomes of the hospital's transplant program (which involves both nephrologists and transplant surgeons).
  • Hospital Facilities: Ensure the hospital has adequate diagnostic capabilities (labs, imaging, biopsy) and support services (dietitian, social worker).
