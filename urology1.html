Choosing a Urologist:

  • Identify Your Condition: Kidney stones, prostate issues (BPH or cancer), bladder problems (cancer, incontinence), male infertility, erectile dysfunction, kidney cancer, urinary tract infections, or a child's urological condition?
  • Sub-specialty Focus: For complex issues like cancer, reconstruction, infertility, or pediatric problems, seeking a urologist specializing in that specific area is highly recommended. Robotic surgery is a tool; look for expertise in treating your specific condition *using* robotics if applicable.
  • Diagnosis: Get an accurate diagnosis first, possibly from a GP or general urologist, who can then refer you to the appropriate sub-specialist if needed.
  • Hospital Facilities: Major surgeries (cancer, transplant, complex reconstruction) require hospitals with excellent infrastructure, ICU support, and multidisciplinary teams.
  • Experience & Volume: Especially for surgical procedures, consider the surgeon's experience and the hospital's volume for that specific operation.
  • Second Opinion: Often wise for major diagnoses like cancer or significant surgeries.
