A representative list of recognized practitioners. Not ranked by efficacy. Scientific evidence lacking for many conditions. Verify BUMS/MD (Unani) qualifications independently.

Crucial Considerations Regarding Unani Medicine in India:

  • Regulation: Unani is regulated by NCISM. Qualified practitioners hold BUMS/MD (Unani) degrees.
  • Scientific Standing: Rigorous scientific validation (by modern standards) is lacking for many classical Unani treatments and concepts.
  • Safety & Quality: Medicine quality can vary; potential contaminants (like heavy metals) exist. Use reputable sources and inform your medical doctor.
  • "Top" is Subjective: Recognition is based on experience, affiliations, lineage, etc., not proven efficacy compared to conventional medicine.
  • Medical Consultation is Vital: Always consult a qualified medical doctor (MBBS/MD) for diagnosis and treatment of serious conditions. Unani should only be complementary and discussed with your doctor. Never replace conventional treatment for serious conditions solely with Unani medicine.

Choosing a Unani Practitioner:

  • Verify Qualifications: Ensure BUMS minimum, ideally MD-Unani, from an NCISM-recognized university.
  • Source of Medicines: Prefer licensed Unani pharmacies. Be cautious with mineral preparations (Kushtas).
  • Diagnosis: Always get diagnosis for serious conditions from an MBBS/MD doctor first.
  • Transparency & Claims: Be wary of unsubstantiated cure-all claims for serious diseases.
  • Integration: Discuss Unani treatments with your conventional medical doctor.
  • Hygiene (for Regimental Therapy): Ensure strict hygiene for procedures like Hijama, Fasd, Leeching.

Prioritize safety by consulting qualified medical doctors for diagnosis and management of significant health conditions.
